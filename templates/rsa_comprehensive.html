RSA (Rivest–Shamir–Adleman)

Comprehensive Explanation

RSA is an asymmetric encryption algorithm used for secure data transmission. It relies on a public-private key pair generated from two large prime numbers. RSA’s security is based on the difficulty of factoring these large numbers.

Encryption Process:

  • Key Generation: Two primes, p and q, are chosen and multiplied to produce n. A public exponent e is selected, and the private key d is calculated.
  • Encryption: The plaintext message is transformed into a numeric form, raised to the power of e, and taken modulo n.
  • Decryption: The ciphertext is raised to the power of d, modulo n, returning the original message.

Security Features:

RSA’s large key sizes (typically 2048 bits or higher) provide robust security, especially for digital signatures and secure key exchange.
