Unicode Cipher

Comprehensive Explanation

The Unicode Cipher method is a flexible encryption approach that shifts characters across the Unicode spectrum. Unlike the Caesar Cipher, which is limited to alphabetic shifts, this cipher can shift any character, making it useful for messages that include symbols and non-alphabetic characters.

Process:

Each character's Unicode value is shifted by a specific amount, potentially wrapping within a range of Unicode values to maintain readability.

Unique Features:

This cipher accommodates a wide range of characters, though it lacks the mathematical robustness of more advanced ciphers like RSA or AES.
