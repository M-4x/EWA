DES (Data Encryption Standard)

Comprehensive Explanation

DES (Data Encryption Standard) was one of the earliest symmetric block ciphers, operating on 64-bit blocks with a 56-bit key.

Encryption Process:

  • Initial Permutation: Bits are rearranged according to a set pattern.
  • Feistel Rounds: Each half of the data block undergoes substitution and permutation using subkeys.
  • Final Permutation: After 16 rounds, a final permutation produces the encrypted result.

Limitations:

Due to its 56-bit key, DES is vulnerable to brute-force attacks, so it has been largely replaced by AES and 3DES.
